History of Karate

Karate, meaning "empty hand," is a martial art that developed on the Ryukyu Islands (now Okinawa, Japan) from indigenous fighting methods and Chinese kenpo. It was influenced by various martial arts, including Fujian White Crane, Shaolin Kung Fu, and indigenous Ryukyuan martial arts.

In the 17th century, weapons were banned by invading Japanese forces, leading to the development of karate as a means of self-defense. The art was further refined and systematized in the early 20th century by masters like Gichin Funakoshi, who introduced karate to mainland Japan.

Today, karate is practiced worldwide and is recognized as an Olympic sport. It emphasizes discipline, respect, and self-improvement alongside physical techniques.

The New Karate

Modern Training Methods

Today's karate incorporates modern training techniques, scientific approaches to conditioning, and sports science principles to enhance performance while maintaining traditional values.

Olympic Recognition

Karate made its Olympic debut at the Tokyo 2020 Games, showcasing the sport's evolution from traditional martial art to competitive discipline with global recognition.

Philosophy & Mindset

The new karate emphasizes mental fortitude, emotional intelligence, and character development alongside physical techniques, reflecting its deeper philosophical roots.

Different Techniques

Offensive Style

Master powerful strikes, punches, and kicks designed to overwhelm opponents with precision and force. Learn techniques like:

  • • Straight punch (Choku-zuki)
  • • Front kick (Mae-geri)
  • • Roundhouse kick (Mawashi-geri)
  • • Knife-hand strike (Shuto-uchi)

Jake Oh

Known for his devastating straight punches and aggressive fighting style. His offensive techniques are precise and overwhelming.

Defensive Style

Develop blocking techniques, evasive movements, and counter-attacks to neutralize threats effectively. Key techniques include:

  • • High block (Age-uke)
  • • Low block (Gedan-barai)
  • • Inside block (Uchi-uke)
  • • Evasion (Tai-sabaki)

A master of defensive techniques with exceptional reflexes and counter-attacks. His ability to read opponents is unparalleled.
